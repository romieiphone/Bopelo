About Us

 “The true meaning of life is to plant trees, under whose Shade you do not expect to sit.” – Nelson Henderson
Going by the inspirational words of Nelson Henderson about the value of making a difference, in 2018 BOPELO is established and started the journey for empowering hope and changing lives that will emphasize socialization and life-skills. People
Bopelo will have a number of programs that will adapt to the necessary expansion. The current centre, in Johannesburg, that will open in 2018 in future to open various satellite facilities across South Africa, Africa and across the world. 

Our Journey

Lindiwe Tylampi, the Founder of Bopelo was born and breed in a small mining village in the North West. She was raised by her Father who was a miner and Mother, a housewife and has a younger brother. Irrespective of their background her father is her inspiration who grew them up strong and independent.
Lindiwe came to Johannesburg to pursue her dreams and for higher studies. To meet the ends she worked at every possible place and every possible work like in Restaurants, doing housekeeping and about any job she could find. Regardless of my situation I managed to help people who were in need more than her while she thanked the almighty Giver to life and Hope, to instil faith and unconditional love towards everyone and to every challenge she went through for she knew there was no day that went pass without learning and growing from that situation, whether it be good or bad. These lessons of life broadened her vision and perspective to life and hence Bopelo came to life. The essence of knowing that nothing is impossible in life when you have Hope, and we at Bopelo try to teach this on a daily basis.

Bopelo's Goals

Encourage acts of volunteerism and community service.

To serve as an international example that shares resources, perform research and do community services for the special-needs population.

Establish and maintain relationships with various academic institutions for doing research programmes and ensuring that the information evolves into educational programs and developmental activities. 

Bopelo's Programs

Digital Literacy

Digital literacy session where a volunteer can teach the rural people on using phone and making use of basic technology like phones and computers. 

Ukulda (food) 

Linking small and big restaurants, hotels and tuck shops with the orphanages and old age homes to offer them food and nutrition. 

Basic Law

Take a session on basic law and its understanding so that rural people cannot be misled.

Welfare and improvements

Awareness sessions on motivation and transformation to make people life ready. 

Smiling Kids – Oral Health Awareness Program

To bring in the oral health awareness amongst the rural communities

Smiling Kids – Mental Health Awareness Program

To bring in the mental health awareness amongst the rural communities

Waste and Sanitation Management

Awareness sessions on waste management and how each household do it in their capacity, at least segregating diverse types of waste like food, plastic etc.  

Umcosco (Nutrition)

Invite nurse and doctors to have regular nutrition-based check-up for small children and provide awareness sessions. 

 Cyber Hygiene

The youth of our country is socially active. Cyber Hygiene is must for all as we should be safe when opening a link or visiting a website. This initiative aims at creating digital hygiene and making them aware of the pros and cons of the digital world.

Garbage to Gold

Hotels, schools and universities produces lots of food waste daily. Have awareness sessions to use these waste foods by turning them into compost and use it in gardens and small farms. 

Hygiene

Awareness session with the children explaining to them the need of washing hands and the proper manner of doing so and other hygiene related initiatives. 

General Diet

The public is not much aware of the benefits of a good healthy diet. This initiative aims to make people more aware of a healthy lifestyle. A health awareness campaign for proper dietary and nutritional values among low income group specifically targeting women.
